You chose to go to the Tavern

You enter the Tavern, to your surpirse it is decently filled. The townsfolk are so busy with their conversations they don't notice you-an outsider- walk in. You take a seat at the bar. The bartender asks what you fancy. Stumpt, you order what you see everyone else drinking: a large beer. You've never drank alcohol before but you figured why not, you just escaped certain death after all.

The more you drink the more confident in your social skills you become. To continue with this night of firsts, you ask a nearby group-beaming with laughter- if you could join them. They allow you to sit with them and thus begins a long night of drinking,socialization,ending with a watch party. You black out afterthe show and after a couple more beers.

You wake in the morning, your head is spinning and you have a sour taste in your mouth. A mixture of throw up and Lager.Your alone aside from the bartender cleaning up after a rowdy night. He speaks to you "Your friends wanted to give this to you, you were prattling pretty hard during your drunken stupor" he says with a sly smile.

He slides a napkin with messy but intelligible writing, it reads:

The Tavern
I’m on my lonesome
The night tempest never ceases
My hands are as stiff as a rose gum

The frost has me blinded
My search will have to wait
I focus on finding safeguard, my attention is undivided

I look ahead and see in bits and pieces
The loveliest little glowing tavern in a village
My pace expeditiously increases

Upon entering, the warmth overwhelmed me
The veiled light dims my vision
I feel, this, is where I’m supposed to be

The place is crowded, I wander to find a seat
“Can I get you something sir?” says the barkeep
Eagerly I say “Anything warm would help me feel complete”

As I wander about, looking to occupy my time
A small projector sits in the corner, preparing to be used
People huddle around, “Show is going to start!” someone chimed

We all settle down, agreeing to the unspoken troth
Transfixed on the screen, the images go in all directions
The beam of light turns us all into moths

The show is over, the crowd disperses for discourse
I leave to ponder on my own
“What did you think?” I’m asked, stopping me midcourse

The patron and I spoke of the show, a conversation most profound
A fellow patron wished to join in as well
Contrary to popular belief three is not a crowd

By the time it was after hours we were four, talking strong
Who knew such complete strangers can have much in common
Who knew what I was searching for would be there all along

Later on, we parted ways, having traded information to keep in touch
A small but important gesture of goodwill
Something that, during these times, you can’t find much

I awake, this journey has now come to its end
For even though we are all strangers
Something tells me that I have found what I was looking for… Friends!

At the bottom you see the signatures of the people you got to know of last night. You lay the napkin down with a melancholic smile. While it fills you with great warmth that you have made new friends, you also rememeber a time when you thought you would be alone forever. Look at how far you've come now. The light pours through the window, shining on you for the first time in a while. A new day awaits.

You got the Tavern ending!
